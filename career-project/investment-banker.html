Job Description

Investment bankers are people that make deals for other companies they also give advice. If a company wants to raise money the investment banker job is to find other people to invest in their company. Investment bankers also act as financial advisors and help companies navigate mergers and acquisitions

Core Tasks

  • Use spreadsheet programs to build financial models
  • Work under the supervision of an associate or vice-president
  • Research and analyze companies Help prepare presentations for companies Maintain databases Interact with clients Bring in new business Generate creative financing ideas for clients Call clients on a regular basis Supervise analysts, associates, and vice-presidents Handle the firm's administrative and managerial duties

Workplace Conditions

  • Work for banks or investment dealers
  • Work indoors in offices
  • 50 to 60 hours a week, with more during busy periods May travel to meet with clients Often talk to clients Working in a competitive industry can be stressful
